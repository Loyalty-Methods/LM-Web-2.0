Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about promotions, discounting, and budget management.

RCX provides multiple layers of margin protection. Define stacking rules that specify which promotions can combine and which are mutually exclusive. Set up exclusion groups to prevent specific offers from running together. Configure margin floors that automatically cap discounts when they exceed thresholds.

The best-offer arbitration engine can be configured to prioritize margin protection, member value, or a weighted balance of both. Every discount decision is logged with full audit trail so you can analyze patterns and adjust rules over time.

Yes. Partner enablement is a core capability. Each promotion can be tagged with funding allocations from multiple sources—your brand, CPG partners, franchisees, or any combination. RCX tracks every earn and burn by funding source automatically.

Settlement reporting breaks down liability by partner with configurable billing cycles. Partners can have their own budget caps, redemption limits, and approval workflows independent of your primary program controls.

Control groups are built into the promotion configuration. When you create a campaign, specify what percentage of eligible members should be held out as a control group. RCX automatically randomizes selection while ensuring statistical validity.

Measurement dashboards show promoted vs. control group performance on key metrics: transaction frequency, average ticket, category penetration, and incremental revenue. Statistical significance indicators tell you when you have enough data to draw conclusions.

Budget controls can be configured for soft or hard caps. Hard caps immediately pause the promotion when the budget is exhausted—no additional redemptions are allowed. Soft caps trigger alerts to stakeholders while allowing the promotion to continue, giving you time to decide whether to extend budget.

Real-time dashboards show burn rate and projected exhaustion dates. Automated alerts fire at configurable thresholds (e.g., 50%, 75%, 90% of budget consumed). Budget extension workflows let authorized users add funds without recreating the campaign.

Absolutely. Every promotion uses the Four W's framework: Who (member segments), Where (locations, regions, outlet lists), When (date ranges, day-of-week, hour-of-day), and What (products, categories, transaction types). Flash offers simply use tight time windows.

Flash promotions can include countdown visibility in member-facing channels, first-N-redemptions caps, and geofenced activation. Launch a "double points for the next 2 hours" offer from the admin console and it's live across all channels within seconds.
