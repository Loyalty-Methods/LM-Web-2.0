Partner Enablement

Native partner management capabilities to onboard, govern, and scale coalition programs, earn/burn partnerships, and data integrations. Each partner operates as its own micro-program with segregated rules, funding limits, and finance-grade traceability.

100 Partners. 100 Different Agreements.

Partnerships are where loyalty programs get complicated. 100 partners means 100 different agreements, 100 different operations, 100 different data formats. Credit card companies, banks, airlines don't do real time. They stick to batch files. Every partner sends something slightly different: CSV, XML, JSON, fixed-width, custom layouts. Point-to-point integrations don't scale. You need a systematic way to handle all of that.

Partnership Models

Native Support for Every Partnership Model

Configure and manage any partnership arrangement through the same rules engine.

Coalition Partners

Shared currency, shared ecosystem. Multiple brands contributing to and drawing from a unified loyalty currency. Each partner maintains its own rules while members see a single program.

Earn Partners

External activity, your points. Members earn your currency through partner transactions. Credit cards, affiliates, co-brands. Attribution tracks back to source for billing.

Burn Partners

Redemption beyond your catalog. Members spend points at partner locations or for partner rewards. Reimbursement calculations track automatically.

Data Partners

Enrich your member profile. Receive data feeds from partners to enhance segmentation and personalization. Activity flows through the same rules engine as first-party data.

Experience Partners

Value beyond transactions. Exclusive access, priority treatment, partner perks. Benefits tied to tier or segment that extend into partner ecosystems.

Co-Funded Promotions

Shared investment, unified experience. Partners contribute funding to joint promotions. Allocation, billing, and settlement tracked per party. Members see a single offer.

Micro-Programs

Each Partner as a Program Within the Program

Every partner operates as its own micro-program within RCX. Segregated rules, access control, and agreements per partner. Partner activity is evaluated, governed, and measured using the same enterprise execution model as your own first-party transactions. The platform handles eligibility, earning outcomes, conversions, and redemptions per partner, with reimbursement calculations that include date-relative accuracy for back-dated processing when rates change.

  • Unique partner rules per location, brand, or agreement
  • Partner transactions processed real-time through rules engine
  • Role-based access control isolates partner visibility
  • Billing parameters attached to rules, partners, locations, and objects
  • Reimbursement calculations with date-relative accuracy

FeedXChange Standardizes the Chaos

FeedXChange was designed out of frustration with building individual point-to-point integrations. Everyone has a slightly different format. CSV, XML, JSON, fixed-width, custom layouts. Point-to-point integrations don't scale. FeedXChange is a batch gateway that handles the complexity so your team doesn't have to rebuild every partner connection from scratch.

Sub-Balance Tracking

Points Are Not the Same

Points can be tagged by source and tracked at sub-balance level. "These are Partner A points, these are Partner B points" in the same balance. The member sees one balance. Finance sees attribution by source. When a redemption happens, you control which bucket it counts against. Individual sources can show deficits while the overall balance remains positive. The customer doesn't see that. But you can see it at a sub level for billing, P&L, and partner settlement.

  • Source tagging by partner, location, channel, promotion
  • Sub-balance tracking invisible to member, visible to finance
  • Redemption attribution controls which bucket is consumed
  • Negative sub-balances supported for granular accounting
  • Full lineage from accrual to redemption for audit

Multiple Integration Modes. Simultaneously.

REST APIs (Synchronous)
