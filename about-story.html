About Loyalty Methods

We learned loyalty in production, then engineered a better way forward.

We solve the hardest problems where enterprise loyalty spans systems, teams, and scale.

Our Story

We didn't start with a platform. We started inside real loyalty programs.

We began by integrating, migrating, and operating systems where downtime wasn't an option. Over time, a pattern became clear: enterprise loyalty doesn't live in one system. It spans POS, payments, digital channels, partner platforms, and data infrastructure. Most tools were rigid or stitched together, forcing brands to adapt their business to the technology. So we made a different choice. We engineered ReactorCX to operate as the loyalty decision layer inside the enterprise, integrating cleanly across systems, executing real-time logic at scale, and supporting change over time without disruption.

We built ReactorCX to operate inside the enterprise, not around it.

2012

Founded to solve real enterprise problems

Loyalty Methods was founded by a team that had spent years inside enterprise programs. They saw loyalty platforms fail not because of missing features but because they couldn't survive real-world deployment conditions.

2016

ReactorCX architecture defined

A headless, event-driven, API-first loyalty engine designed to operate inside existing enterprise ecosystems rather than replace them.

2018

ReactorCX enters controlled production

ReactorCX was deployed alongside legacy systems, processing live traffic in parallel to validate correctness, scale, and determinism.

February 2020: The Button Push

7-Eleven Cutover

ReactorCX transitioned from parallel operation to primary execution for 7-Eleven across the U.S. and Canada. Legacy systems remained live until parity was proven, then traffic was switched in a controlled cutover with no downtime and no customer impact.
